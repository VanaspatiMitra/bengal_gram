About the Crop:

Bengal Gram Pusa 362 is a high-yielding chickpea variety that thrives in well-drained loamy to clay loam soils with a pH of 6.0 to 7.5. It’s a robust, drought-resistant crop requiring around 300 mm of water throughout its 110-120 day growing period. Pusa 362 typically yields 8-10 quintals per acre under optimal conditions. The crop’s water efficiency and ability to grow in less fertile soils make it cost-effective. Profit margins are favorable, with net profits ranging from ₹15,000 to ₹25,000 per acre, depending on market prices and input costs.

  • Soil Composition: well-drained loamy to clay loam soils with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 0.21 to 0.25 feet
  • Potential Dangers: Climate Variability, Pest Infestation, Waterlogging
  • Fertilizers: Phosphorus, Zinc Sulphate, Potassium, Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC,Trichoderma viride,Mancozeb 75 WP, Dimethoate 30 EC
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹33,000 to ₹53,000
